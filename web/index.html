The Biology Behind the Simulation

Understanding the science of stem cell regeneration.

🔄

Self-Renewal

Stem cells maintain the population by dividing symmetrically — producing two identical daughter stem cells. This is controlled by the Division Probability parameter.

🔀

Differentiation

Asymmetric division produces one stem cell and one specialised cell. The Differentiation Probability governs how frequently this occurs.

⏳

Cellular Ageing

All cells accumulate age each cycle. Once a cell exceeds its Max Cell Age, it undergoes apoptosis (programmed cell death).

💀

Apoptosis

Specialised cells also face stochastic death each cycle. The Specialised Death Rate models natural cell turnover in tissues.

🧪

Multi-Lineage

In multi-lineage mode, stem cells can differentiate into Hematopoietic, Neural, or Mesenchymal cells — mirroring real multipotent stem cell behaviour.

💊

Growth Factors

The Growth Factor slider simulates therapeutic intervention — amplifying division probability to model the effect of growth-factor treatment on tissue regeneration.

from stem_cell_sim import simulate_regeneration

# Cherry Computer Ltd. — Stem Cell Sim v2.0
results = simulate_regeneration(
    cycles=20,
    initial_stem_cells=8,
    division_prob=0.65,
    differentiation_prob=0.45,
    growth_factor=1.3,
    multi_lineage=True,
    seed=42,
)

print(results["Stem"][-1])   # final stem count
